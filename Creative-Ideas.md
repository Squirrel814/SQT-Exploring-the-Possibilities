### 2. Core Components

#### A. Holidays & Lore Calendar (Fully Updated)

The **SQT Holidays & Lore Calendar** forms the foundational rhythm of the entire Living Grove ecosystem. All holidays are strictly SQT-timed (based on day-of-lunation and lunation count) so they remain independent of solar/Gregorian dates and naturally drift over time.

The calendar is built on three layers:

1. **Recurring Every Lunation** — The steady daily/weekly heartbeat of the Grove.
2. **4 Major Lunation Events** — Quarterly milestones with a light **Hero’s Journey** narrative arc across the SQT year.
3. **Rare Periodic Events** — Surprise, high-impact moments that add depth and memorability.

All holidays feed directly into **The Messenger’s Circuit** (the Daily Oracle), influencing journal prompts, mood boards, story seeds, art prompts, and foraging ideas.

---

### 1. Recurring Every Lunation (The Core Daily Rhythm)

These five holidays occur on fixed days within **every** 19-day lunation. They create a consistent weekly-like structure that users can learn and anticipate.

| Day(s) of Lunation | Holiday                              | Theme                              | Frequency      | Core Feeling                  |
|--------------------|--------------------------------------|------------------------------------|----------------|-------------------------------|
| **1**              | Whisperwake of the First Message     | Messenger energy & new beginnings  | Every lunation | Fresh, attentive, intentional |
| **4–5**            | The Discerned Hoard                  | Wise preparation & curation        | Every lunation | Focused, discerning, practical|
| **7**              | Leybridge Threading                  | Incremental bridge-building        | Every lunation | Steady, connective, patient   |
| **13–14**          | Burrowstill Knowing                  | Strategic rest & inner wisdom      | Every lunation | Quiet, reflective, restorative|
| **17**             | Chatterseed Scatter                  | Playful generous sharing           | Every lunation | Generous, social, releasing   |

**Detailed Descriptions:**

- **Whisperwake of the First Message (Day 1)**  
  The lunation begins with Ratatoskr delivering the first whisper of the cycle. This day emphasizes clarity of intention and attentive listening before action begins.

- **The Discerned Hoard (Days 4–5)**  
  Early in the lunation, focus shifts to wise selection. Not everything is worth caching. This is the time to review priorities and decide what truly deserves energy and attention.

- **Leybridge Threading (Day 7)**  
  Mid-lunation energy supports steady, incremental progress. Small, consistent actions (“one pebble”) build connections across projects, ideas, and relationships.

- **Burrowstill Knowing (Days 13–14)**  
  As the lunation deepens, the emphasis moves inward. This is protected time for reflection, integration, and allowing insight to settle without forcing output.

- **Chatterseed Scatter (Day 17)**  
  Near the end of the lunation, energy turns outward again. This is a day for generous sharing — scattering ideas, wins, and resources without attachment.

### 2. The 4 Major Lunation Events (Hero’s Journey Arc)

These four events occur on the **last day of Lunations 3, 6, 9, and 12**. Together they form a gentle **Hero’s Journey** structure across the full SQT year, giving the calendar emotional and narrative progression.

| Lunation | Event Name                    | Hero’s Journey Stage       | Purpose                                      | Frequency |
|----------|-------------------------------|----------------------------|----------------------------------------------|---------|
| 3        | Hoardkeeper’s Joyful Reckoning| The Call to Adventure      | Inventory, release, and prepare              | 1×/year |
| 6        | **The Shadow Trial**          | Trials & Challenges        | Face resistance, tension, or creative blocks | 1×/year |
| 9        | **The Burrow Rebirth**        | Reward & Transformation    | Integrate lessons and emerge changed         | 1×/year |
| 12       | The Messenger’s Circuit Complete | The Return              | Full-year integration and returning wisdom   | 1×/year |

**Proposed Lore & Purpose for Each Major Event:**

- **Hoardkeeper’s Joyful Reckoning (Lunation 3)**  
  Before the deeper journey begins, the squirrel takes joyful stock of what has been cached so far. This is not solemn accounting — it is a playful celebration of what to keep, what to release, and what to carry forward.

- **The Shadow Trial (Lunation 6)**  
  Midway through the year, the squirrel encounters resistance, confusion, or creative difficulty. This event honors the necessary struggle and the clever adaptations that emerge when usual paths are obscured.

- **The Burrow Rebirth (Lunation 9)**  
  After working through challenges, a period of deep rest and transformation occurs. Old structures may fall away so something stronger and more authentic can emerge.

- **The Messenger’s Circuit Complete (Lunation 12)**  
  At the end of the full SQT year, Ratatoskr returns from the complete circuit. This is the moment of integration — bringing together everything learned across the twelve lunations and preparing for the next great cycle.

These four events are designed to feel more ceremonial and emotionally significant than the recurring holidays. **The Messenger’s Circuit** (the Daily Oracle) expands significantly on these days, delivering richer prompts and stronger narrative framing.

### 3. The Messenger’s Circuit (The Daily Oracle)

**Official Name:** The Messenger’s Circuit

#### Philosophy & Purpose

**The Messenger’s Circuit** is the central daily ritual and creative engine of the SQT Living Grove ecosystem. It serves as a personal daily message from the Grove — delivered through Ratatoskr’s perspective — that helps the user reflect, create, and act in alignment with SQT time.

Rather than being a generic prompt generator, The Messenger’s Circuit is designed to feel like receiving a thoughtful transmission from the living world of SQT. It blends three core functions into one cohesive experience:

- **Mindful Reflection** (journaling and inner awareness)
- **Creative Generation** (mood boards, story seeds, and art prompts)
- **Practical Action** (foraging ideas and project-aware micro-tasks)

The name **The Messenger’s Circuit** reflects both the daily nature of the practice and the larger cyclical journey of wisdom moving through the Grove (especially visible in the year-end event *The Messenger’s Circuit Complete*).

---

#### Core Experience

Every day, The Messenger’s Circuit delivers a **thematically unified bundle** that feels like a single, coherent message rather than disconnected parts. The tone is **mythic yet grounded** — poetic enough to inspire, but practical enough to be useful in real creative and productive work.

The experience is intentionally cyclical:
- It begins with **receiving** (listening to the whisper/message of the day).
- It moves through **reflection and creation**.
- It ends with **action** (a small, meaningful step or “forage”).

This mirrors the squirrel’s natural rhythm of listening, caching, and moving.

---

#### Inputs

The Messenger’s Circuit pulls from the following sources:

- **Live SQT Data**: Current lunation and day-of-lunation (from the unified engine).
- **Active Holiday Context**: The current recurring holiday, and any active Major Lunation Event or Rare Periodic Event.
- **User Project Priorities** (optional but recommended): A lightweight list of active projects so the Oracle can make suggestions relevant to the user’s actual work.
- **Previous Context** (future enhancement): Light memory of recent days or lunations for continuity.

---

#### Output Bundle Structure

When The Messenger’s Circuit runs, it generates a single, cohesive daily package containing the following elements:

| Element                    | Description                                                                 | Purpose                                      | Example Tone                  |
|---------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Journal Prompt**        | A reflective question tied to the day’s energy and current holiday         | Deepen self-awareness and cyclical thinking  | Thoughtful, slightly mythic   |
| **Mood Board**            | Color palette + imagery descriptors + ready-to-use image generation prompt + atmosphere notes | Visual and atmospheric inspiration           | Sensory and evocative         |
| **Story Seed**            | A short narrative starter featuring a squirrel protagonist                 | Creative writing / world-building spark      | Whimsical but meaningful      |
| **Art Prompt**            | A detailed, ready-to-use prompt in the **SQT Grove Style**                 | High-quality generative image creation       | Consistent visual language    |
| **Foraging Idea**         | One tailored micro-action or side-quest (general, holiday-specific, or project-aware) | Practical movement and productivity          | Actionable and encouraging    |

The bundle is designed so all five elements feel like they belong to the **same message** from the Grove on that particular day.

---

#### Tone & Narrative Voice

The Messenger’s Circuit speaks with a consistent voice that can be described as:

- **Wise but not preachy**
- **Playful but not silly**
- **Mythic but grounded in real creative and life processes**

It often uses gentle messenger or circuit metaphors (“What message arrives for you today?”, “What are you carrying forward on this part of the circuit?”) without becoming overly mystical.

On ordinary days, the tone is supportive and clarifying.  
On **Major Lunation Events** (especially the four Hero’s Journey milestones), the tone becomes noticeably more ceremonial and archetypal.

---

#### Integration with Holidays & Events

The current holiday or event is the primary flavoring agent for The Messenger’s Circuit:

- **Recurring Lunation Holidays** subtly shift the flavor of the prompts and foraging ideas (e.g., more outward and generous on Chatterseed Scatter, more inward on Burrowstill Knowing).
- **The 4 Major Lunation Events** trigger significantly richer, more expansive versions of the bundle. These days often include stronger narrative framing and deeper reflective questions.
- **Rare Periodic Events** (such as Shadowforage Eclipse or The Whisper Storm) can override or layer additional themes onto the daily output when active.

This creates natural rhythm and emotional peaks throughout the SQT year.

---

#### Modes & Variations

While the core bundle remains consistent, The Messenger’s Circuit can operate in different modes depending on user preference or context:

- **Standard Mode** — The full balanced bundle (recommended default).
- **Minimal Whisper Mode** — Lighter version focused on one strong prompt + one foraging idea (for busy or low-energy days).
- **Project-Deep Mode** — Heavily tailored toward the user’s current project priorities.
- **Storytelling Mode** — Emphasizes evolving story seeds and long-term narrative continuity (especially powerful when using the Evolving Protagonist approach).
- **Major Event Mode** — Automatically activates richer, more ceremonial output on the four quarterly milestones.

Users should eventually be able to select or let the system suggest the most appropriate mode for the day.

---

#### Technical & Data Considerations

- The core logic lives in a modular prompt engine (maintained by Jasper) that combines:
  - Live SQT data
  - Holiday data from `sqt-holidays.json`
  - Theme and tone data from `sqt-themes.json`
  - Optional user project context
- All outputs should remain relatively lightweight so they can be delivered quickly through Discord, web components, and CLI.
- The system should support both **on-demand generation** and **scheduled daily delivery** (e.g., via Discord or email).

---

#### Design Notes

**Visual / Aesthetic**  
All generative outputs (especially mood boards and art prompts) must follow the **SQT Grove Style Guide** — whimsical storybook illustration style with strong natural textures, warm forest palette, recurring acorn/leyline/Ratatoskr motifs, and holiday-appropriate accent colors.

**Mechanical**  
The Messenger’s Circuit must stay in sync with the unified SQT engine and holiday system. It should feel reliable and consistent while still feeling alive and responsive to the current moment in the SQT year.

**Education Impact**  
Regular use teaches cyclical thinking, prompt engineering, and the value of small daily creative and reflective practices. The Hero’s Journey framing across the four major events offers a light mythic structure for personal growth and storytelling.

- **Crystal**: Visual style guide, mood board system, and example outputs.
- **Jasper**: Core prompt engine, JSON schemas, and technical delivery.
- **Damian**: Timestamp hygiene and “Squirrel Ops” framing for productivity and creative process documentation.

### Community Widgets Layer

The **Community Widgets Layer** serves as the **front door** and social layer of the entire SQT Living Grove ecosystem. While **The Messenger’s Circuit** provides the deep daily experience and the Holidays provide the underlying rhythm, the widgets make these elements accessible, visible, and participatory for both existing users and newcomers.

Their primary purpose is to lower the barrier to engagement. Someone should be able to have a meaningful interaction with SQT in under 30 seconds through a widget, while also having clear pathways into deeper experiences (such as running the full Messenger’s Circuit bundle or participating in community storytelling).

---
#### Core Philosophy

The widgets are designed around four principles:

- **Accessibility First**: Easy to use with minimal friction.
- **Social & Participatory**: They should enable light community creation and connection, not just passive consumption.
- **Consistent Ecosystem Voice**: All widgets should feel like part of the same Living Grove (visually, tonally, and thematically).
- **Low-Pressure Participation**: Light gamification and optional engagement. The goal is joy and connection, not obligation or grind.

---
#### The Three Core Widgets

##### 1. Discord Bot — “Ratatoskr Grove Messenger”

This is currently the most powerful community-facing widget.

**Primary Commands:**
- `/circuit` — Delivers today’s Messenger’s Circuit output (or a teaser with a link to the full bundle).
- `/forage` — Returns a tailored foraging/side-quest idea (general, holiday-specific, or project-aware).
- `/lore-drop` — Allows users to submit original squirrel stories, reflections, or creative work tied to the current SQT day or holiday (goes into a moderated queue).
- `/holiday` or `/event` — Shows details about today’s holiday or any active Major/Rare event.

**Key Features:**
- **Ratatoskr Relay**: A collaborative storytelling feature where the bot starts a story seed based on the current day or holiday, and users continue it in a thread. Especially powerful during the 4 Major Lunation Events.
- **Holiday Events**: Special bot behavior and prompts during the four major events (e.g., guided reflection during Hoardkeeper’s Joyful Reckoning or creative challenges during The Shadow Trial).
- **Light Gamification**: Cosmetic rewards only (roles such as “First Nibble Forager,” “Leyline Listener,” or “Circuit Walker”). No heavy progression systems.
- **Curated Facts**: Occasional responses include respectful, credited squirrel folklore from different cultures to enrich the Grove’s mythic layer.

**Moderation & Safety**: All user-generated content (`/lore-drop`) goes through a simple approval queue. Rate limiting and basic input sanitization are required.

##### 2. Web Component — `<sqt-grove-clock>`

A lightweight, embeddable web component that can be placed on personal sites, project pages, Notion workspaces, or the main SQT dashboard.

**Core Features:**
- Live display of current SQT time + active holiday indicator.
- Mini calendar grid showing upcoming holidays and major events.
- Clickable elements that open a modal with:
  - Full lore for the current holiday or event.
  - A teaser of today’s Messenger’s Circuit.
  - One-click button to generate or view the full daily bundle.
- “Share” functionality (copy prompt, share to Discord, or generate image).

**Design Requirements:**
- Fully accessible (ARIA labels, keyboard navigation, high contrast support).
- Theming support so it can match different sites while staying within the SQT Grove visual language.
- Self-contained (minimal dependencies).

This widget is ideal for passive visibility and quick access to The Messenger’s Circuit.

##### 3. VS Code Integration

A status bar item and command palette integration aimed at developers and heavy note-takers.

**Features:**
- Status bar displays current SQT time + short holiday indicator (e.g., “Day 7 • Leybridge Threading”).
- Command: **“Insert Messenger’s Circuit Bundle”** — Pastes today’s full themed output (journal prompt + story seed + foraging idea) directly into the active file as a comment block or markdown section.
- Hover tooltips on the status bar can show a short lore snippet or today’s foraging suggestion.

This turns The Messenger’s Circuit into a natural part of a developer’s or writer’s daily workflow and log-keeping practice.

#### Detailed Data & Content Flow

**1. Time → Meaning Layer**
- The unified SQT engine provides the current lunation and day.
- This data is matched against `sqt-holidays.json` to determine:
  - Any active **recurring holiday**
  - Whether today is one of the **4 Major Lunation Events**
  - Whether a **Rare Periodic Event** is active
- `sqt-themes.json` then supplies the appropriate tone, color palette, and narrative flavor for that day or event.

**2. Meaning → Daily Practice Layer**
- The Messenger’s Circuit engine receives the combined context (SQT stamp + holiday/event + themes).
- It generates a single, thematically consistent daily bundle.
- On ordinary days, the bundle is balanced and supportive.
- On **Major Lunation Events**, the bundle becomes significantly richer and more archetypal (aligned with the Hero’s Journey stage).
- On **Rare Periodic Events**, special thematic layers or variations are applied.

**3. Daily Practice → Delivery Layer**
- The generated bundle from The Messenger’s Circuit is made available through multiple interfaces:
  - Discord Bot (`/circuit`)
  - Web Component (one-click generation in modal)
  - VS Code (direct insertion into notes/logs)
  - Future: Web dashboard, CLI, Obsidian plugin, etc.

**4. Community Feedback Loop**
- Users can take outputs from The Messenger’s Circuit and contribute back into the ecosystem via:
  - `/lore-drop` in Discord
  - Sharing through the web component
  - Ratatoskr Relay collaborative storytelling
- Approved community contributions can later influence future Oracle outputs or be highlighted during relevant holidays (with credit).

#### User Experience Flows

**Normal Day Flow (Example: Day 7 – Leybridge Threading)**

1. User checks the **Web Component** or Discord status → sees they are on Leybridge Threading.
2. They run **The Messenger’s Circuit** (via bot, web, or VS Code).
3. They receive a balanced bundle focused on steady progress and small connective actions.
4. They may complete the suggested foraging idea and optionally log it.
5. If inspired, they might share a reflection or creation via `/lore-drop`.

**Major Event Flow (Example: Lunation 12 – The Messenger’s Circuit Complete)**

1. The system recognizes this is the final day of the SQT year.
2. All widgets surface special indicators and richer lore for the event.
3. **The Messenger’s Circuit** generates a significantly deeper, more ceremonial bundle with strong themes of integration and return.
4. The Discord bot may initiate or encourage a **Ratatoskr Relay** focused on year-end reflection.
5. The experience feels like a meaningful yearly ritual rather than a normal daily check-in.

This contrast between ordinary days and major events creates natural emotional rhythm across the year.

---

#### Community Participation Loop

The widgets are designed to turn passive users into active participants over time:

```
The Messenger’s Circuit 
        → Generates personal creative/reflective content
                → User shares via widgets (/lore-drop or share button)
                        → Content enters community layer (with moderation)
                                → Approved content can inspire future Oracle outputs
                                        → Strengthens sense of shared Grove culture
```

This loop helps the ecosystem feel alive and co-created rather than purely top-down.

---

#### Technical Integration Principles

- **Single Source of Truth**: `sqt-holidays.json` and `sqt-themes.json` are the central sources for holiday logic and tonal guidance. All widgets and The Messenger’s Circuit read from these files.
- **Loose Coupling with Clear Contracts**: The Oracle engine exposes a clean interface so it can be called by Discord, web components, VS Code, or future tools without tight dependencies.
- **Progressive Enhancement**: Basic time + holiday display works even without The Messenger’s Circuit. Richer features (full bundles, generative prompts) are layered on top.
- **SQT Purity**: No component should introduce solar calendar dependencies. All timing and triggering logic must remain internal to SQT.
